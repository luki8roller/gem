All About Water

The most essential substance on Earth — covering our planet, shaping life, and powering everything from oceans to a single human cell.

Why Water Matters

Water is a clear, tasteless liquid made of two hydrogen atoms bonded to one oxygen atom (H₂O). It is the only common substance found naturally as a solid, liquid, and gas, and it makes life as we know it possible.

The Water Cycle

Water constantly moves between oceans, the atmosphere, and land through evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and runoff — recycling the same water that has existed for billions of years.

Freshwater & Oceans

About 97% of Earth's water is in the oceans. Less than 3% is freshwater, and most of that is locked in glaciers and ice caps, leaving only a tiny fraction available for drinking and agriculture.

Water in the Body

The adult human body is roughly 60% water. It transports nutrients, regulates temperature, cushions joints, and supports nearly every chemical reaction that keeps us alive.

Unique Properties

Water expands when it freezes, has a remarkably high heat capacity, and acts as a near-universal solvent. These properties stabilize our climate and make biology possible.

Conservation

Fixing leaks, taking shorter showers, and choosing water-efficient appliances can dramatically reduce household water use — protecting a resource that billions still struggle to access.

Water & Climate

Oceans absorb heat and carbon dioxide, moderating global temperatures. Changes to rainfall, glaciers, and sea levels are among the clearest signals of a shifting climate.
